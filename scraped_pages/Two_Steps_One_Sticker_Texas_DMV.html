Two Steps, One Sticker.

About Single Sticker

As a result of House Bill 2305 passed during the 83rd Texas Legislature (2013), the State of Texas transitioned to a "Two Steps, One Sticker" vehicle inspection and registration program. Your registration sticker serves as combined proof of registration and inspection, if an inspection is required to register your vehicle. In order to renew on time, your vehicle must pass an emissions inspection no earlier than 90 days before your registration expires, if you live in certain Texas counties.

Who's at the Wheel

The implementation of "Two Steps, One Sticker" is a joint effort of three state agencies as required by state law:

  • The Texas Department of Motor Vehicles (TxDMV)
    TxDMV administers the state's vehicle registration program.
  • The Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS)
    DPS regulates the state's vehicle inspection stations and administers the safety inspection program.
  • The Texas Commission on Environmental Quality (TCEQ)
    TCEQ administers the state's vehicle emissions testing program.

Registration Renewal Process

Here's a more detailed overview of how renewing your vehicle registration works.

Before issuing your registration renewal notice, TxDMV checks the state inspection database for your last emissions inspection if you reside in Brazoria, Collin, Dallas, Denton, El Paso, Ellis, Fort Bend, Galveston, Harris, Johnson, Kaufman, Montgomery, Parker, Rockwall, Tarrant, Travis, or Williamson County.

There are multiple options for renewing your vehicle registration:

  • Online
    If you are required to obtain a passing emissions inspection and the passing inspection is listed in the inspection database available at MyTXCar.org, you can complete your renewal online and your new registration sticker will be mailed to you.
    If you are required to obtain a passing emissions inspection and the passing inspection is not in the inspection database and you are unable to certify that the vehicle is out-of-state, you will need to obtain a passing vehicle inspection or visit your local county tax office and present your Vehicle Inspection Report (VIR) to complete your renewal.
    Renew Your Registration Online
  • By Mail
    If you are required to obtain a passing emissions inspection and the passing inspection is not listed in the inspection database available at MyTXCar.org, include a copy of your Vehicle Inspection Report (VIR) with your registration renewal notice and your local county tax office will process your renewal. Your new registration sticker will be mailed to you.
    If you are required to obtain a passing emissions inspection and have not your renewal will not be processed. Your county will request that you provide verification of a passing emissions inspection before processing your registration.
  • In Person
    Vehicle registration and renewal may be completed at your local county tax office or at any county willing to accept the application. If you are required to obtain a passing emissions inspection and the passing inspection is in the inspection database, the clerk will proceed with your renewal.

Most emissions inspection records are available in near real time, but there may be instances when the record is not immediately available. If your vehicle passed emissions inspection but the system is unable to verify it electronically, you may present the passing Vehicle Inspection Report (VIR) you received from the inspection station to the county tax office at the time of renewal.

If you need to show proof of emissions inspection to complete your vehicle's registration renewal, visit MyTXCar.org to download, display electronically, or print a copy of the document. Your emissions inspection will be verified manually, and you will be permitted to renew your vehicle registration.

If you are renewing your registration at a county subcontractor location, such as your local grocery store, they may not accept your VIR as proof of emissions inspection. Each county determines whether their subcontractors can verify emissions inspection via VIR. If a subcontractor is not permitted to verify via VIR, you will be directed to the county tax office to complete your renewal.

Special Circumstances

Out-of-State Motorists

If your vehicle is currently out-of-state and you are unable to complete a Texas vehicle emissions inspection in order to renew your registration, you may self-certify that the vehicle is out-of-state and will be permitted to register online, by mail, or in person.

If you renew your vehicle registration using the out-of-state self-certification option, a remark will be placed on your vehicle record indicating that an emissions inspection is still due if your vehicle is registered in Brazoria, Collin, Dallas, Denton, El Paso, Ellis, Fort Bend, Galveston, Harris, Johnson, Kaufman, Montgomery, Parker, Rockwall, Tarrant, Travis, or Williamson counties. Once you return to the state, you must complete a Texas vehicle emissions inspection within three days of arrival at your home, duty station, or destination.

It is very important that you keep the VIR issued after completing your emissions inspection in case you are stopped by law enforcement before the remark is removed from your vehicle record, which can take two business days. The remark will be removed only upon payment of the state's portion of the Emissions Inspection Fee and verification of the emissions inspection at your county tax office.

Multi-Year Registration

Owners registering a new vehicle will pay a $16.75 Inspection Replacement Fee to cover the first two years. You will receive a registration renewal notice from TxDMV prior to your registration expiration instructing you to pass an emissions inspection if your vehicle is registered in Brazoria, Collin, Dallas, Denton, El Paso, Ellis, Fort Bend, Galveston, Harris, Johnson, Kaufman, Montgomery, Parker, Rockwall, Tarrant, Travis, or Williamson counties.

A 'VERIFY INSPECTION' remark will be placed on the motor vehicle record for failure to pass a Texas emissions inspection and pay the state portion of the Emissions Inspection Fee to your local county tax office. This remark can result in law enforcement citation.

New Texas Residents

Your vehicle must be registered within 30 days after you move to Texas. Your vehicle must pass an emissions inspection, prior to registration if your vehicle is registered in Brazoria, Collin, Dallas, Denton, El Paso, Ellis, Fort Bend, Galveston, Harris, Johnson, Kaufman, Montgomery, Parker, Rockwall, Tarrant, Travis, or Williamson counties. The state's portion of the Emissions Inspection Fee is due at the time of registration. For more information on new resident registration requirements, please visit the TxDMV website.

Title Transfer and Private Vehicle Sales

If you purchase a used vehicle via private party sale, you are still required to pay the state's portion of the Inspection Replacement Fee, which is due at the time of registration. The vehicle must have a passing emissions inspection, if you reside in a designated emissions county, and pay the designated Emissions Inspection Fee before it can be titled and registered. Designated emissions counties are Brazoria, Collin, Dallas, Denton, El Paso, Ellis, Fort Bend, Galveston, Harris, Johnson, Kaufman, Montgomery, Parker, Rockwall, Tarrant, Travis, and Williamson.

Trailer Inspection Requirements

As of January 1, 2025, Texas trailer owners are no longer required to obtain a safety inspection prior to registration but are still required to pay the Inspection Replacement Fee at time of registration.
